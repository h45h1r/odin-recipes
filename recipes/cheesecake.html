Cheesecake

Indulge in the delightful combination of white chocolate and raspberries with this exquisite White Chocolate Raspberry Cheesecake recipe. This dessert is reminiscent of the ones you would find in a high-end restaurant, making it perfect for special occasions or when you want to treat yourself. Garnish with white chocolate curls for an extra touch of elegance.

The White Chocolate Raspberry Cheesecake is a true decadent delight that combines the richness of white chocolate with the tangy sweetness of raspberries. The crust, made with chocolate cookie crumbs, melted butter, and sugar, provides a deliciously crunchy base that perfectly complements the creamy cheesecake filling. It sets the stage for the layers of indulgence to come. The luscious filling is a velvety blend of cream cheese, white chocolate chips, and a hint of vanilla extract. The white chocolate is melted with half-and-half, creating a smooth and luxurious texture that melts in your mouth with every bite. The raspberry sauce, made from frozen raspberries, sugar, and cornstarch, adds a burst of vibrant flavor and a beautiful marbled effect to the cheesecake. With its sweet and tangy notes, the raspberry sauce balances out the richness of the white chocolate, creating a harmonious symphony of flavors. This White Chocolate Raspberry Cheesecake is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes. The combination of creamy white chocolate, vibrant raspberry sauce, and a chocolate cookie crust creates an exquisite presentation that is sure to impress. Whether you're celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in a luxurious dessert, this cheesecake is a showstopper that will leave you and your guests craving more.

Ingredients

    Crust:

  • 1 cup chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar

    • Sauce:

  • 1 (10 ounce) package frozen raspberries
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch

    • Cheesecake:

  • 2 cups white chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup half-and-half cream
  • 3 (8 ounce) packages cream chees, softened
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Make crust: Mix together cookie crumbs, melted butter, and sugar in a medium bowl. Press mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  2. Make sauce: Combine raspberries, water, sugar, and cornstarch in a saucepan. Bring to a boil and continue boiling until sauce is thick, about 5 minutes. Strain through a mesh strainer to remove seeds.
  3. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
  4. Make cheesecake: Melt white chocolate chips with half-and-half in a metal bowl over a pan of simmering water; stir occasionally until smooth.
  5. Mix together cream cheese and sugar in a large bowl until smooth. Beat in eggs one at a time. Blend in vanilla and melted white chocolate mixture. Pour 1/2 of the batter over crust. Spoon 3 tablespoons raspberry sauce over batter. Pour remaining cheesecake batter on top. Spoon 3 tablespoons raspberry sauce over batter. Swirl batter with the tip of a knife to create a marbled effect.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until filling is set, 55 to 60 minutes. Cool cheesecake at room temperature, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate until thoroughly chilled, 8 hours to overnight.
  7. Remove cheesecake from the pan and serve with remaining raspberry sauce.
